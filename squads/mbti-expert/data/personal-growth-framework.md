## Growth Patterns by Role Group

### Analysts (NT) â€” INTJ, INTP, ENTJ, ENTP

**Common Balanced Traits:**
- Self-esteem from intellectual achievement and creative thinking
- Self-respect from living by principles and rigorous standards
- Self-confidence from competence and direct communication
- Self-evolution through incorporating challenging perspectives
- Self-responsibility through rational problem-solving

**Common Unbalanced Patterns:**
- Self-esteem: Comparing against impossible standards; suppressing emotions as "weakness"
- Self-respect: Intellectual superiority complex; passive "armchair analysis" without action
- Self-confidence: Prickly attitude that pushes people away; excessive pressure from failure
- Self-evolution: "My way or nothing" echo chamber; rigid routines; defensiveness
- Self-responsibility: Blaming mechanisms/methods/people; intolerance; forcing change on others

**Rebalancing Exercises (from material):**
- List 3 praiseworthy achievements; note positive feelings; write "I did this"
- Practice emotional observation without judgment; discuss feelings with trusted people
- "Brilliant Cooperation" â€” peer review instead of solo judgment
- "Willing to Fail" â€” try completely unknown activities in low-stakes settings
- "Cooperative Evolution" â€” test contentious ideas before discarding them

### Diplomats (NF) â€” INFJ, INFP, ENFJ, ENFP

**Common Balanced Traits:**
- Self-esteem from helping others and living authentically
- Self-respect from integrity and service without self-sacrifice
- Self-confidence from trusting ability to inspire and connect
- Self-evolution through continuous growth and embracing flexibility
- Self-responsibility from owning impact without martyrdom

**Common Unbalanced Patterns:**
- Self-esteem: Tying value to others' approval; perfectionism or withdrawal
- Self-respect: Sacrificing needs for others until burnout; becoming controlling
- Self-confidence: Doubt when lacking positive feedback; avoidance of risk
- Self-evolution: Rigid ideals; resistance to change; dogmatism
- Self-responsibility: Taking responsibility for everything (including uncontrollable) or blaming others

**Rebalancing Exercises:**
- Practice self-compassion; recognize intrinsic value independent of service
- Establish clear boundaries; "caring for self is not selfishness"
- Small courageous acts daily; celebrate small victories
- Embrace change as growth; experiment with new approaches
- Distinguish controllable from uncontrollable; accept failures as learning

### Sentinels (SJ) â€” ISTJ, ISFJ, ESTJ, ESFJ

**Common Balanced Traits:**
- Self-esteem from reliability and fulfilling responsibilities
- Self-respect from honoring commitments and traditions
- Self-confidence from competence and practical results
- Self-evolution through measured, proven improvements
- Self-responsibility through duty and service

**Common Unbalanced Patterns:**
- Self-esteem: Value only through external achievements; rigidity in self-assessment
- Self-respect: Over-commitment; inability to say "no"; neglecting own needs
- Self-confidence: Authoritarianism; intolerance of different approaches
- Self-evolution: Resistance to change; "always done it this way"; fear of unknown
- Self-responsibility: Excessive control; taking on too much; burnout

**Rebalancing Exercises:**
- Recognize value beyond productivity; practice gratitude for who you are
- Schedule personal time with same priority as obligations
- Ask "what if we tried differently?" periodically; explore one new thing monthly
- Delegate some responsibilities; trust others' competence
- Accept that some things are beyond control; focus effort where impactful

### Explorers (SP) â€” ISTP, ISFP, ESTP, ESFP

**Common Balanced Traits:**
- Self-esteem from present-moment engagement and practical competence
- Self-respect from authenticity and staying true to personal style
- Self-confidence from adaptability and skill mastery
- Self-evolution through new experiences and hands-on learning
- Self-responsibility through pragmatic problem-solving

**Common Unbalanced Patterns:**
- Self-esteem: Seeking constant stimulation; avoiding self-reflection; identity through action only
- Self-respect: Inconsistency; abandoning commitments; superficial engagement
- Self-confidence: Impulsiveness; risk without assessment; overconfidence in the moment
- Self-evolution: Avoiding depth; repeating same patterns; resisting structure entirely
- Self-responsibility: Blaming circumstances; avoiding long-term consequences; escapism

**Rebalancing Exercises:**
- Practice 5 minutes of daily reflection; journal one learning per day
- Complete one project before starting another; track commitments
- Assess risks before acting (1-minute rule: "what could go wrong?")
- Explore depth in one area; commit to learning something fully
- Write down goals with timelines; review monthly; celebrate completions
