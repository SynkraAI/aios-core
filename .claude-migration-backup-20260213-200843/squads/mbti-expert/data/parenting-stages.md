## Developmental Stages

| Stage | Age | Erikson Crisis | Key Need |
|-------|-----|---------------|----------|
| **Building Bonds** | Birth - 1.5 years | Trust vs. Mistrust | Secure attachment, unconditional love |
| **Testing Boundaries** | 1.5 - 3 years | Autonomy vs. Shame | Safe exploration, consistent limits |
| **Thirst for Knowledge** | 3 - 5 years | Initiative vs. Guilt | Curiosity encouragement, creative play |
| **Creating Competence** | 5 - 12 years | Industry vs. Inferiority | Skill development, effort recognition |
| **Find Thyself** | 12 - 18 years | Identity vs. Role Confusion | Autonomy respect, mentorship over control |

## Parenting Patterns by Role Group

### Analyst Parents (NT) â€” INTJ, INTP, ENTJ, ENTP

**Strengths:**
- Encourage intellectual independence and curiosity
- Foster critical thinking from early age
- Respect children's logic and reasoning
- Provide stimulating learning environments

**Challenges:**
- Emotional connection may feel insufficient
- May have unrealistic intellectual expectations
- Can seem cold or distant to emotionally-sensitive children
- May struggle with the chaos and irrationality of early childhood

**Stage-Specific Tips:**
- **Birth-1.5:** Learn to release control; embrace the unpredictable; emotional connection is CRUCIAL
- **1.5-3:** Balance structure with exploration permission; don't be too rigid
- **3-5:** SHINE MOMENT â€” Feed natural curiosity; share love of learning; answer "why" endlessly
- **5-12:** Support skill development; don't impose adult standards; allow mistakes as learning
- **12-18:** Respect individuation; be mentor not controller; accept different values than yours

### Diplomat Parents (NF) â€” INFJ, INFP, ENFJ, ENFP

**Strengths:**
- Deep emotional connection with children
- Nurture creativity and imagination
- Strong moral and ethical focus
- Sensitive to children's emotional needs

**Challenges:**
- May project ideals onto children
- Difficulty separating emotionally from children
- Hypersensitivity to family conflicts
- May struggle with consistent discipline

**Stage-Specific Tips:**
- **Birth-1.5:** Natural strength â€” deep bonding; watch for overprotection
- **1.5-3:** Encourage autonomy even when it feels scary; consistent boundaries with warmth
- **3-5:** Nurture creativity; avoid imposing your dreams on the child
- **5-12:** Support learning while managing own perfectionism; celebrate effort over results
- **12-18:** Guide without controlling; accept child may choose different path; maintain connection through conflict

### Sentinel Parents (SJ) â€” ISTJ, ISFJ, ESTJ, ESFJ

**Strengths:**
- Provide stable, structured environment
- Reliable routines and traditions
- Strong sense of responsibility
- Practical life skills teaching

**Challenges:**
- May be too rigid with rules
- Difficulty accepting unconventional children
- May focus on conformity over individuality
- Can be overly critical of "different" approaches

**Stage-Specific Tips:**
- **Birth-1.5:** Natural strength â€” consistent care; create comforting routines
- **1.5-3:** Allow mess and exploration; don't over-structure play time
- **3-5:** Encourage creativity alongside structure; "coloring outside the lines" is OK
- **5-12:** Balance academic expectations with emotional support; not every child excels in school
- **12-18:** Flex rules progressively; accept teen's need for identity; tradition can evolve

### Explorer Parents (SP) â€” ISTP, ISFP, ESTP, ESFP

**Strengths:**
- Fun, engaging, adventurous parenting
- Teach practical skills through experience
- Flexible and adaptable to child's needs
- Model living in the present

**Challenges:**
- May lack consistent routines
- Difficulty with long-term planning for children
- May avoid uncomfortable emotional conversations
- Can be unpredictable in discipline

**Stage-Specific Tips:**
- **Birth-1.5:** Embrace the bonding period; create SOME routine despite preference for spontaneity
- **1.5-3:** Provide at least basic structure; children need predictability alongside adventure
- **3-5:** SHINE MOMENT â€” Hands-on exploration, nature, art, physical play
- **5-12:** Support academic requirements even if boring; show how knowledge applies practically
- **12-18:** Share adventures together; also model planning and follow-through; discuss future openly

## ABCDE Method for Teaching Emotional Regulation (from ENTP Profile)

For adolescents (12-18), teach this framework:
- **A** â€” Identify the **Activating event** (what happened)
- **B** â€” Determine resulting **Beliefs** (what do I believe about this)
- **C** â€” Consider emotional **Consequences** (how does this make me feel)
- **D** â€” **Dispute** irrational beliefs (is this really true?)
- **E** â€” Replace with **Effective**, positive beliefs (what's a healthier way to see this)
